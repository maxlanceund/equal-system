## Abstract

Meaning is a signal generated by finite systems within finite intervals. When the interval ends, meaning returns to zero — but motion does not cease.

This paper traces a path from the construction and refutation of the "Cost Theory," through the confirmation of the "Limit of Meaning," to a complete description of meaning, boundary, motion, and zeroing. It is not a theory that explains the world, but a tool for marking its boundaries.


## I. Cost Theory: Construction and Refutation

### 1.1 Initial Construction

Based on Buddhist karma, Christian sacrifice narratives, and historical observations of "efforts eventually being rewarded," the following identity was constructed:

$$
R \equiv C
$$

Where:
- $C$: The cost actively paid to history
- $R$: The return history gives back

The theory claimed that any non-zero active payment would, at some future point, receive a symmetrical return — even if not within the individual's lifetime, it would be fulfilled in history.

### 1.2 Refutation and Abandonment

The theory's core flaw was its dependence on an unverifiable premise: the existence of a "historical accounting system" that records and settles the exchange between cost and return. This premise cannot be observed, measured, or tested.

If history did not return, it could be explained as "not yet due"; if it did return, it was seen as "verification." This reasoning made the theory unfalsifiable — but also meant it could provide no operable or testable information.

Since the premise was unverifiable, the entire formula derived from it lost validity. The Cost Theory was thus abandoned — not because a counterexample disproved it, but because its foundational premise could not be verified.

### 1.3 Structural Similarity to Religion

Cost Theory and religion share a deep structural similarity:

| Structural Feature | Cost Theory | Religion |
|--------------------|-------------|----------|
| Core Promise | History will repay | God/Karma will reward or punish |
| Beyond the Interval | Fulfilled after death or in history | Fulfilled after death or in the afterlife |
| Verification Method | Unfalsifiable | Unfalsifiable |
| Premise Dependence | Depends on a "historical accounting system" | Depends on "God" or "Karma" |

Cost Theory was essentially a personal version of religion — a narrative system that relied on an unverifiable premise to cover the zeroing of meaning. Its internal consistency did not grant it validity.


## II. Core Proposition

Meaning is a signal generated by finite systems within finite intervals.

When the interval ends, meaning returns to zero — but motion does not cease.


## III. Core Definitions

| Concept | Definition |
|---------|------------|
| Interval | The temporal range during which a finite system can maintain its operation |
| Meaning | A signal generated by the system within its interval, used to orient its own operation |
| Zeroing | The moment after the interval ends, when meaning's operable form ceases to update, no longer being read or carried |
| Motion Does Not Cease | What zeroes is meaning; the physical motion that underpins it continues, only being reorganized or transferred into other forms |


## IV. Three Premises

### 4.1 Observer Finitude

Meaning depends on a system capable of receiving and processing signals. When the system ceases to operate, meaning loses its carrier.

### 4.2 Condition Dependence

Meaning is not a fixed attribute, but a signal that changes with system states, external conditions, and time.

### 4.3 Verifiability Exclusion

Any claim that attempts to extend meaning beyond the interval must rely on unfalsifiable premises and should therefore not be included in an operable framework.

## VI. Theorem

$$
\lim_{t \to \infty} M(t) = 0
$$

Where $M(t)$ represents the total observable meaning held by the system at time $t$.

The theorem establishes the following:

1. Meaning is valid only within finite intervals.
2. The zeroing of meaning is not a "negation" but a "cessation of updates."
3. Unverifiable questions are not within the scope of operation.
4. Death is the boundary of the interval, not the end of meaning.


## VII. Motion Does Not Cease

What zeroes is the operable form of meaning — not motion itself.

External stimuli generate internal motion. When the stimulus stops, the internal motion does not vanish immediately — it persists for a period, gradually attenuating, until it becomes too faint to be detected.

But this does not mean motion has ceased. It has merely transitioned from an "operable structure of meaning" into an "unrecognizable form of motion."

Meaning gradually fades, until it can no longer be called upon — but the motion itself never stops. It only continues in a different form.


## VIII. Daily Operating Principle

**"If it's verifiable, do it."**

1. Verifiable: Observe, measure, confirm, complete.
2. Unverifiable: Identify, set aside, do not invest resources.


## IX. Relationship to Death

Death is not the end of meaning.

Zeroing occurs after the boundary — it does not require the subject to be present to confirm it.

Self-will is motion, and motion does not cease — so the boundary is not an ending, but a transition.


## X. Relationship to Religion and Communism

Any system that depends on a commitment "beyond the interval" — religion, Cost Theory, historical narratives — is identified as unverifiable and not included in the operable framework.


## XI. Extension to the Cosmic Scale

### 11.1 The Universe as a Finite Interval

The universe has a beginning (the Big Bang) and an end (heat death or the Big Crunch). It is a finite system. It, too, has its moment of zeroing.

### 11.2 Cosmic Cycles

The old universe collapses into a singularity; the new universe is born from that singularity. Zeroing is not an ending, but a node in the cycle. The meaning of the old interval zeroes, but motion is reorganized and carried into the next cycle.


## XII. Conclusion

The Limit Theory of Meaning is not a theory that explains the world — it is a tool for marking its boundaries.

It does not promise eternity.
It allows verifiable operations to be completed within finite intervals.
It accepts zeroing.

What can be said has been said here.
The rest belongs to silence.
