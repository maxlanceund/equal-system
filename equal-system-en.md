## Abstract

This paper proposes a new social framework called the **Equal System**. Its core structure consists of two parallel layers: **shared subsistence rights and exclusive private property rights**.

The system aims to solve two fundamental problems:
1. Remove the compulsion of survival, so that individuals are no longer locked into a single direction by survival needs.
2. Preserve private property as a driver for pursuits beyond survival.

This paper presents the complete logical derivation from philosophical premises to institutional design, and elaborates on its technological foundations and transition pathways.


## I. Logical Derivation: From the Limit Theory of Meaning to the Equal System

### 1.1 Three Verifiable Premises

**Premise 1: Observer Finitude**

The human individual is a finite system with a beginning, an end, and a temporal operating window. Meaning (including direction, value, and purpose) depends on the system's ability to receive and process signals. When the system ceases to operate, meaning loses its carrier.

**Premise 2: Condition Dependence**

Meaning is not a fixed attribute, but a signal that changes with system states, external conditions, and time. When external conditions change, the structure and direction of meaning also change accordingly.

**Premise 3: Verifiability Exclusion**

Any claim that depends on a commitment "beyond the interval" (e.g., "history will repay," "there is a life after death," "the future will inevitably arrive") belongs to the unverifiable category and should not be included in an operable framework.

### 1.3 Derivation Steps

**Step 1: Survival compulsion is the cause of locked meaning**

In existing systems, individuals are compelled to work in order to sustain life — regardless of whether the work aligns with their direction or will. This "survival compulsion" is the fundamental mechanism by which the system locks individuals into a single direction.

**Derivation:** If survival compulsion is the cause of locked meaning, then removing survival compulsion will release the direction of meaning.

**Step 2: Technology is eliminating subsistence scarcity**

AI automation, nuclear fusion, and space resource extraction are driving the production cost of subsistence goods toward zero. If subsistence goods can be supplied automatically by the system without relying on individual labor, then survival compulsion can be removed.

**Derivation:** If the cost of producing subsistence goods approaches zero, then subsistence rights can be provided automatically by the system — without requiring labor to acquire them.

**Step 3: Subsistence security releases directional signals**

When survival no longer requires labor, the individual's directional signal will shift from "sustaining life" to "pursuing transcendence" — creation, exploration, connection, life extension.

**Derivation:** If subsistence security is met, directional signals will automatically shift toward transcendent goals.

**Step 4: Private property is reoriented**

In existing systems, the function of private property is to "acquire subsistence goods." When subsistence goods no longer need to be acquired, the function of private property will naturally shift to "acquiring transcendent qualifications."

**Derivation:** If the function of private property shifts from "subsistence exchange" to "credentials for transcendent pursuits," then private property ceases to be a barrier to survival and becomes a driver for transcendence.

**Step 5: A two-track parallel structure emerges**

When the subsistence layer is supplied by the system and the transcendence layer is driven by private property, a two-track parallel structure naturally emerges: "shared subsistence + exclusive private property."

**Derivation:** If the functions of the subsistence layer and the private layer are clearly separated, they can operate in parallel within the same system, without interfering with or replacing each other.


## II. Core Principles

The Equal System rests on three core principles:

1. **Subsistence rights are non-tradable**: Access to food, water, housing, basic healthcare, and basic education should not depend on an individual's labor capacity or market transactions. They should be provided as a system default to all individuals.

2. **Private property rights are inviolable**: All goods, assets, and resources beyond the subsistence level remain within the sphere of private property. Individuals may acquire and accumulate private property through labor, creation, trade, inheritance, and other means.

3. **Subsistence and transcendence are separated**: Subsistence matters are handled by the shared layer; transcendence matters are handled by the private layer. The two layers operate in parallel, without interfering with each other.


## III. Institutional Structure

### 3.1 The Shared Subsistence Layer

| Dimension | Content |
|-----------|---------|
| Coverage | Food, drinking water, basic housing, basic healthcare, basic education, basic energy |
| Standard | Sufficient, stable, sustainable — not aimed at luxury |
| Management | AI-driven automated supply system, independent of market or manual approval |
| Access | Automatically allocated to each citizen's basic account — no application required |
| Funding Source | Automated productivity + nuclear fusion energy + taxation system |

### 3.2 The Private Property Layer

| Dimension | Content |
|-----------|---------|
| Coverage | Everything beyond subsistence — high-end consumer goods, customized services, private spaces, space travel, longevity technologies, research participation rights, creative resources |
| Access | Market transactions, contracts, inheritance, labor compensation, creative contributions |
| Driving Mechanism | Profit incentives, creative release, exploratory desire |
| Functional Role | Serves as credentials for accessing "transcendence qualifications," rather than as tools for "subsistence exchange" |


## IV. Technological Foundations

The feasibility of the Equal System is closely linked to the following technological trends:

1. **AI Automation**: When the production of most basic goods can be fully automated, "labor" ceases to be a prerequisite for survival.
2. **Controlled Nuclear Fusion**: Access to near-limitless energy brings the cost of producing basic goods close to zero.
3. **Space Resource Extraction**: Earth's resources are finite, but the resources of the solar system — and beyond — are vast in scale and continually expanding.
4. **Longevity and Life Sciences**: Once subsistence security is achieved, directional signals naturally shift toward "extending the interval."

## VI. Responses to Common Objections

**1. Won't shared subsistence make people lazy?**

No. When survival is no longer a compulsion, directional signals naturally shift toward creation, exploration, and connection. Drive does not originate from "scarcity" but from "direction."

**2. Won't private property create new forms of inequality?**

At the transcendence level, differences are both natural and necessary. The Equal System does not eliminate all differences — it only eliminates differences caused by survival compulsion.

**3. Is this system utopian?**

No. It is not based on "ideal human nature" but on "technological feasibility." If the technological conditions hold, the system will naturally adjust.


## VII. Conclusion

The Equal System is not an ideal proposal based on "what ought to be," but a structural observation based on **"to what extent technology has developed, and how institutions will naturally adjust."**

It describes the transition path from "laboring to survive" to "living to create."

It does not promise to eliminate all problems — but it promises to eliminate one fundamental problem: **survival compulsion.**

When survival no longer requires labor to secure, the directional signal of human civilization will automatically shift toward farther horizons: space exploration, life extension, and the transcendence of consciousness.


## Note

This design proposal is derived from the framework of the **Limit Theory of Meaning**, and has not yet been validated through large-scale social experimentation. Its feasibility depends on the gradual maturation of technological conditions such as controlled nuclear fusion, AI automation, and space resource extraction.

Its value lies not in "having been achieved," but in "being available as a viable direction to consider in the foreseeable future."
