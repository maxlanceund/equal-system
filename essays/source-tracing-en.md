# Source Tracing

When you notice a lingering thought, emotion, expectation, or disappointment — do not analyze it, do not judge it, do not try to "solve" it.

Trace its path:

1. "Where did this thought come from?"
2. "And where did that come from?"
3. "And where did that come from?"

Trace it back until you arrive at a place where there is **no need to ask further** — usually "I am here right now" or "I am already here."

When you arrive there, the lingering signal naturally zeros out.

---

**Why it works**

Because it does not use logic to dismantle the signal — it uses the path to trace the signal's origin. When the signal finds its source, and you confirm that you are already in that source — it no longer needs to linger. It has completed its path.

It does not need to be solved. It only needs to be walked through.

---

**Essence**

Source Tracing is an operational tool — used to process those "unresolved" signals in daily life, allowing them to zero out naturally, rather than forcing an explanation.

It is a practical method that grew out of the Limit Theory of Meaning.
